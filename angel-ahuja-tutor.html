About me

I teach psychology.
I use it to teach everything else.

I'm finishing my BA in Psychology at Simon Fraser University — with a minor in Human Development and Counselling Psychology — maintaining an A average across six years of immersion in the science of how people learn, think, and reason.

That depth isn't just a credential. It's a teaching method. Every session I run is grounded in how memory and learning actually work: retrieval practice, spaced repetition, metacognitive reflection. I adapt to how you think, not just what you need to know.

Writing and critical thinking are my strongest areas. I help students find a clear, confident analytical voice — for psychology essays, sociology papers, philosophy assignments, or anything that asks you to think and argue on the page. I've also worked with students with diverse learning needs, including ADHD and autism, and adapt my teaching style to suit how each individual thinks and processes information best.
